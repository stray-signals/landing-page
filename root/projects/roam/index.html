Introducing
ROAM.
A camera for wanderers & storytellers.
The Idea

Most cameras made for kids are either tablets with a camera app or toy cameras that happen to take photos. ROAM is just a camera. No games, nothing on it to do except take a picture and tell a story.

If it breaks, you can fix it. The parts are standard and replaceable - nothing proprietary, nothing that requires sending it back.

What's Inside
📷
Real Camera
OV2640 sensor. Shoots up to 1600x1200, saves to microSD as JPEG.
🎛️
Tactile Dial
EC11 rotary encoder - click, turn, press. Feels like something.
🎞️
Film Simulations
Dial through looks based on classic films. Kodachrome, Portra, a few others.
📡
No-App Gallery
Hold the dial to spin up a Wi-Fi hotspot. Browse photos in any browser, no app needed.
🖥️
E-Ink Display
Shows the current film simulation and Caroline. Barely uses battery while idle.
🔋
Rechargeable
Built-in LiPo, charges over USB-C. No AA batteries to hunt for.
How You Use It
Single Press
Take a photo - saved instantly to the SD card
Turn Dial
Cycle through film simulations & filters
Hold 3 Seconds
Start the Wi-Fi gallery - browse photos on your phone
Slide Switch
Power on / off - simple as it gets
Status Light
White flash - photo taken
Red - low battery
Green - charging
Blinking blue - starting gallery
Solid blue - device connected

Meet Caroline

Every ROAM has a companion on the e-ink display - a Carolina Wren named Caroline. She reacts to what the camera is doing: still while idle, animated when a photo lands, drooping when the battery gets low. Small, but you'd notice if she wasn't there.
