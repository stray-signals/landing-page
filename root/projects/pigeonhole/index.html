The Idea

Pigeonhole takes the part of portrait photography nobody enjoys - the sorting - and makes it disappear. Photograph each subject on your phone, and by the time you're back at your desk, every photo is already named and waiting in its own folder. No cloud. No subscription. No late night of dragging and dropping.

The Problem It Solves

School photo day. Team headshots. Employee badges. You walk away with three hundred photos and a list of names. Matching them by hand is a day of work. Sending them out to a lab costs a fortune and takes a week. Pigeonhole does it in the room, in minutes, on your own phone and laptop.

How It Works
01
Add Your Subjects
Enter names and group them by class, team, or whatever makes sense for your shoot.
02

Name the shoot - Grade 4 portraits, Staff IDs, whatever the day calls for - and open the camera.
03

04
Deliver
Send the photos to your laptop wirelessly. One folder per person, named and ready to hand off.

Getting Photos to Your Laptop
On your laptop
Open the Pigeonhole desktop app and click "Receive Photos" - a code appears on screen
On your phone
Tap Export, scan the code, and hit Send - that's the whole process
What you get

The Bigger Picture

The devices photographers already carry are more than capable of doing this work. The missing piece was software that got out of the way and let them shoot. Pigeonhole is that software - built for the Tuesday morning school gym, not the software demo room.
